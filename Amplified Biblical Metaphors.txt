Selection
:

Reproduction and Multiplication
: The seed that falls on good ground produces fruit "an hundredfold." In terms of natural selection, those organisms best adapted to their environment will reproduce more, passing on their advantageous traits to a larger number of offspring.
While the primary intent of the parable is spiritual and moral instruction, the analogy to natural selection is an interesting perspective. It underscores the idea that success (whether it's a seed growing into a fruitful plant or an organism surviving to reproduce) often depends on both inherent qualities (like the seed's potential or an organism's genetic traits) and external conditions (the type of soil or the environment).
