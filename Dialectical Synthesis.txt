Synthesis and antithesis are concepts often discussed within the framework of dialectical reasoning, which is a method of argument for resolving disagreement that has been central to Western philosophy since the time of Socrates. Here's how they differ:
Antithesis
is the direct opposite of the thesis. It's the second stage in the dialectical process, representing a contradiction or negation of the initial assertion or thesis.
Synthesis
is the third stage in the dialectical process. It seeks to resolve the conflict between the thesis and antithesis by integrating elements of both into a new, more comprehensive level of truth.
So, in simple terms, while antithesis is about opposition, synthesis is about resolution and integration.
