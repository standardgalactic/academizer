Glossary of Key Terms and Concepts
Capitalist Utopianism
Definition
: An ideological framework within capitalism that promises ultimate fulfillment, purity, and the resolution of all desires, akin to a utopian vision.
Explanation
: According to Helen Rollins, capitalism constructs a fantasy of complete satisfaction that denies intrinsic human lack (see "Lack"). This utopian promise is inherently contradictory, as capitalism cannot fully deliver on its assurances, leading to psychological and social consequences.
Lack
Definition
: A psychoanalytic concept referring to an inherent, unresolvable gap or deficiency within the self, central to human subjectivity.
Explanation
: Drawing from psychoanalytic theory, "lack" represents the universal truth that the human subject is never complete due to an internal void. Capitalism, as described, represses acknowledgment of this lack, promoting a false narrative of achievable wholeness through consumption or success.
Repression of Lack
Definition
: The systemic denial within capitalist ideology of the intrinsic gap or incompleteness within individuals.
Explanation
: Capitalism constructs a narrative that individuals can overcome their internal lack through material or social achievements. This denial, from a psychoanalytic perspective, results in unresolved psychological tensions and societal issues, as the promised fulfillment remains unattainable.
Logic of Opposition
Definition
: A binary framework inherent in capitalist ideology that simplifies complex issues into oppositional categories.
Explanation
: Examples include winners versus losers or consumers versus non-consumers. This binary framing obscures nuanced realities, reinforcing simplistic and divisive thinking that aligns with capitalist structures.
Bad Infinity
Definition
: An endless cycle of unfulfilled desire perpetuated by capitalist systems.
Explanation
: Capitalism incentivizes a continuous loop of desire, where satisfaction is perpetually deferred, creating a "bad infinity" with no resolution. This concept highlights the structural inability of capitalism to fulfill its promises.
Failure to Deliver
Definition
: The inherent inability of capitalism to fulfill its utopian promises, as noted by Marxist theory.
Explanation
: Drawing on Karl Marx, the text argues that capitalism is driven by internal contradictions, preventing it from achieving the fulfillment it promotes. This failure fuels ongoing social and economic tensions.
Psychocinema
Definition
: A theoretical approach proposed by Helen Rollins that views cinema as a psychoanalytic tool for exposing and destabilizing ideological fantasies.
Explanation
: Unlike conventional therapeutic tools that comfort, psychocinema uses film to disturb viewers, shatter illusions, and reveal subjective contradictions, aligning with the disruptive aims of psychoanalysis.
Cinema as Couch
Definition
: A metaphor for cinema's role as a psychoanalytic medium that provokes discomfort rather than reassurance.
Explanation
: Similar to a psychoanalytic session, cinema should unsettle viewers, challenging their ideological assumptions and exposing internal conflicts rather than providing escapist comfort.
Identity over Contradiction
Definition
: A critique of contemporary theory's tendency to reduce psychoanalytic complexity to identity politics.
Explanation
: The text argues that modern interpretations often replace the psychoanalytic focus on internal division ("you're divided") with affirmations of identity ("you're perfect"), oversimplifying human subjectivity and aligning with neoliberal ideologies.
Desire Loop
Definition
: A perpetual cycle of desire reinforced by cinematic narratives within capitalist culture.
Explanation
: Drawing on Lacanian psychoanalysis, cinema perpetuates an infinite chase for completion, feeding into the "bad infinity" of unfulfilled desire central to capitalist ideology.
Master Discourse
Definition
: The dominant ideological framework under which films operate, often reinforcing capitalist values explicitly or subtly.
Explanation
: Movies, as cultural products, frequently propagate or subtly uphold the prevailing ideology of capitalism, shaping audience perceptions in alignment with systemic norms.
Fake Radicalism
Definition
: Criticism of identity-based critiques of capitalism that inadvertently reproduce neoliberal logic.
Explanation
: The text argues that focusing solely on diverse representation or identity politics often fails to challenge capitalism's core structures, instead reinforcing its ideological framework under the guise of radical critique.
True Emancipation
Definition
: Genuine liberation from ideological constraints, achieved by dismantling capitalist fantasies rather than seeking superficial reforms.
Explanation
: The text posits that true emancipation requires breaking free from the utopian fantasies perpetuated by capitalism, prioritizing dialectical engagement with contradictions over mere diversification of representation.
Analyst's Discourse
Definition
: A psychoanalytic approach emphasizing contradiction, dialectics, and discomfort to challenge ideological ease.
Explanation
: Rollins advocates for a return to this discourse in cinema, where films provoke critical reflection on contradictions and resist comforting ideological narratives, fostering deeper awareness of systemic issues.
Deep Screen Look
Definition
: A metaphorical description of cinema's potential as a profound psychoanalytic intervention.
Explanation
: At its best, cinema serves not as a self-help tool but as a medium that confronts viewers with the void of their subjectivity, offering a transformative encounter with the complexities of the self and society.
