It is called the "hard" problem because it is considered difficult to explain or understand using current scientific and philosophical theories. The idea is that while we can explain the physical processes that underlie perception and cognition, we still don't have a full understanding of how these processes give rise to subjective experience. The hard problem of consciousness is often considered separate from the "easy problems" of cognitive science, which refer to the scientific study of perception, attention, memory, and other cognitive processes. These easy problems can be studied and understood using current scientific methods and theories, but the hard problem remains a challenge for many scientists and philosophers.
