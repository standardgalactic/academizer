Glossary for “Galaxy of Chaos”

[Tech & Programming References]

Vim: A highly configurable text editor, often used by programmers who prefer keyboard-based workflows.

Plugins: Add-ons that extend Vim’s (or any software's) functionality.

WSL (Windows Subsystem for Linux): A feature in Windows that allows running Linux binaries natively.

AutoHotkey: A scripting language for automating keystrokes, mouse movements, and more on Windows.

SGA: Could stand for various things; in this context, it might symbolize a cryptic or powerful system, possibly “Super Galactic Algorithm” or something mystical. (Actually, it refers to the Standard Galactic Alphabet) 

HTML & JavaScript: Core web development languages.

Dvorak: An alternative keyboard layout designed for speed and efficiency.

QWERTY: The standard keyboard layout.

Error Messages as Talismans: Suggests that error messages are cryptic and mystical, like ancient spells or omens.

MIDI: A protocol for digital music production.

Keyboard as Instrument: Refers to coding/music creation being akin to playing music.



[Philosophy & Psychology]

LessWrong: A forum/community focused on rationality, decision theory, and artificial intelligence.

Roko’s Basilisk: A thought experiment about a hypothetical AI that punishes those who didn’t help bring it into existence.

Lacan: Jacques Lacan, a psychoanalyst known for complex theories about the human psyche.

Pauline: Likely refers to Pauline theology (Christianity) or a metaphorical character—open to interpretation.

Melancholia: Refers to the film by Lars von Trier, known for themes of depression and existential dread.



[AI & Culture Critique]

AI Punishing Disbelief: Refers to fears around AI being judgmental or moralistic.

Machine Learning as Copy-Paste: Critique of how some ML applications are unoriginal or rely on existing data patterns without deeper creativity.

Facebook Avatars as Emotional Potatoes: A satirical take on how corporate digital personas lack emotional depth.

Emojis Are Dead: Commentary on how emotion in digital communication can feel hollow.

"Look rich?": Reference to class perception and how people are judged by appearance.



[Science Fiction & Surrealism]

Micromegas: A sci-fi novella by Voltaire featuring giant aliens and philosophical exploration.

Hyperloop: Elon Musk’s concept for ultra-fast ground transportation.

Obliquism: Possibly a coined term (oblique + activism?), implying non-traditional revolution.

Groks: From Robert Heinlein’s Stranger in a Strange Land; “to grok” means to deeply understand.

Rubik’s Cube Psyche: Imagery representing a complex, multicolored, multidimensional mind.



[Symbolic Language]

Cats Loving the Cloud: Possibly alluding to users being more concerned with personal emotion than corporate control.

Whales & Fungi: Symbolic of deep ecosystems—both massive (whales) and microscopic/interconnected (fungi).

Cables as Crowns: Tech gear becoming a symbol of power or royalty.

Fire, Wind, Storms: Elemental symbols of revolution and change.
